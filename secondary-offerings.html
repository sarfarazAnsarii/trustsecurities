Debt IPOs

Debt IPOs in the form of non-convertible debentures are fixed-term financial instruments issued by a company to raise capital via debt. Company invites investors to lend them money for a specified period to fund their business requirements. In return, investors are promised a predetermined rate of return called the coupon. Non-convertible debentures can be secured or unsecured.
